Parasites

Parasites, ways of infection and danger of infection

Almost 200 species of helminths can affect a human body.

Helminths are divided into three classes:
- roundworms
- tapeworms
- flukes.
Today the importance of helminthiasis is underestimated, they are difficult to diagnose.
In connection with a higher degree of contamination of soil with helminths eggs, the prevalence of helminthiases in a country is certainly higher than in towns.
Biohelminthes are passed to humans through animals - how toxoplasma from a cat, for example.
Geohelminths spend a certain period of their development in soil, from where they safely come to us with vegetables and greens, such as ascarids for example.
A contagious parasites are passed from an infected person to a person, such as pinworms for example.
Larval and developing forms of helminths make the most destructive effect on a body. After all, adult helminth has, as a rule, a stable location in a body - a larva is able to make difficult trips to the organs, making them painful changes.
The favorite habitat for parasites is the gastrointestinal tract, different species prefer certain parts for their habitation, for example, ascarids - the initial sections of the small intestine, pinworms - the lower parts of the small and initial parts of the large intestine, and the whipworm - the initial section of the large intestine.
Depending on the location parasites are divided into luminal (a parasite lives in the lumen of the hollow organ) and the tissue forms (a parasite is in the thickness of the tissues). Some helminthiases (for example, ascaridosis) in different stages can have both tissue and luminal forms.
Parasites use two ways to penetrate a host organism:
- through skin - an exogenous pathway;
- through the walls of the intestine - an endogenous pathway.
For intestinal parasites, of course, endogenous penetration is more convenient.
Ways of infection with parasites:
- with products: through vegetables, greens, fruits, berries, meat, fish. We ate not completely roasted or boiled meat, we tasted forcemeat, kebab - we can get larvae of pork or beef tapeworm. Fish are not fried or dried not quite right, not salted - here you are larva of opisthorchiasis.
- with air: inhaling dust with eggs or cysts of parasites.
Soil, especially in large cities, is polluted with manure and feces of domestic animals. All this dries up, rises into the air, and we breathe it.
- with the ground: through dirty hands. Soil is one of the important factors in the passing of helminth eggs. Some helminths - geogelminthes (ascarids, whipworms, palisade worms, trichostrongyloidosis, etc.) undergo one of the stages of their development in soil and can remain viable for a long time in it. For example, ascaris eggs can survive in soil under the conditions of the central Russia - 7-8 years, in Central Asia - up to 15 years; eggs of whipworms - from 1 to 3 years.
- with water: through drinking water (even filters do not save) or swimming in ponds, lakes, pools.
- through things: through utensils contaminated with cysts: linens, toys, dishes.
- from sick home or wild animals.
A harmless home shell parakeet can have up to 30, and a cat or dog - from 70 to 300 potentially dangerous species of parasites for humans.
- Today the intrauterine route of infection of a child from a mother has been proved.
Recently, the frequency of calls to doctors about the diagnosis and treatment of parasitic diseases has increased. Unfortunately, this often occurs in the late stages of the disease, when treatment is hard to do or perhaps treatment can be done only by an operative way, when a person has echinococcosis, for example.
Parasites disrupt the functions of organs in which they are localized. For example, with opisthorchiasis, the liver function is impaired: the outflow of bile worsens, the helminths clog the bile ducts, diarrhea is replaced with constipation. A patient complains of pain in the right hypochondrium, loses weight, he has nausea, sometimes vomiting, bitter taste in a mouth, conjunctiva and mucous membranes become icteric. His working capacity decreases, there is an apathy.

Your result

Wash your hands before eating and after going to a toilet. It is also necessary to wash greens, vegetables and fruits that are in contact with soil and can carry geohelminths (that is, "ground" helminths). Do not swim in reservoir that have not been tested by the relevant sanitation services. It is necessary to exclude the use of raw meat and fish, fry it thoroughly boil.

It is necessary to wash hands with a soap everywhere and always to protect yourself from parasites. Wash vegetables and fruits thoroughly. Try not to drink raw water. Do not self-medicate, do not take drugs against parasites without consulting a doctor, because they can be toxic not only for parasites, but for you too. If the presence of a parasite in a body is confirmed, it is worthwhile to look for a good parasitologist, who will prescribe an effective scheme of treatment, because taking one medicine is usually not enough.
