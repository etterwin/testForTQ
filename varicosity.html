Varicose veins

Prevention of varicose veins for a woman-driver

Even unprofessional female drivers often spend several hours a day driving, especially in cities, where traffic jams have long become attributes of everyday life. Therefore it is not surprising that occupational diseases became common for motorists and drivers working for hire.

Long sitting in one position behind the wheel leads to a violation of the outflow of blood through the veins, especially if a driver smokes and is overweight. In particular, hemorrhoids can appear. Use of ointments-phlebotonics is necessary in any case. Visit to a doctor is desirable in such cases. During a break in driving, special attention should be paid to legs and feet of both legs.
Keeping a correct diet should be the constant concern of a driver. After each workout you need to arrange snacks: eat something useful: vegetables, boiled meat, drink tea or coffee.
Proper nutrition and breaks on the road will help avoid hypodynamia, causing varicose veins and other diseases of the cardiovascular system.
Being behind the wheel, a driver is constantly in suspense. So, not to have overpressure a driver needs a full rest, including a pleasant pastime, several hours for sleeping, water procedures and so on.
Female blood vessels are naturally thinner than those of men. And if there is hypodynamia, blood stagnates in them, which, by the way, can cause not only varicose veins of the lower limbs but also hemorrhoids.
To reduce the risk of both disorders while standing in traffic jams, do not waste time - rotate your feet in a circle and roll your foot from heel to toe. Tense muscles of buttocks and then relax. At home, sit with your legs elevated. In the evenings, make warm foot baths and at least once a day take a contrast shower. In the morning and in the evening massage feet with special creams that relieve fatigue and improve the venous blood circulation. And of course, walk more, swim in the pool. And do not forget to see the phlebologist once a year.
